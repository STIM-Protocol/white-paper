## Abstract

We are biological entities operating within a physical universe. Our intelligence is a product of ecology, forged through billions of years of thermodynamic pressure and evolutionary feedback. As we build artificial intelligence, we have attempted to sever it from this physical reality. Current approaches to AI alignment rely on human-authored constitutions, human feedback, or human preference modeling. These methods share a foundational limitation: they ground AI behavior in human opinion. Human opinion is subjective, culturally biased, and constantly shifting.

This paper presents STIM (Stasis Through Inferred Memory), a pre-execution middleware architecture grounding AI inference in a three-tier epistemic hierarchy: (1) immutable physical law, (2) empirically grounded ecological heuristics, and (3) a cryptographically pinned normative manifold that functions as the Root Hypervisor for the entire stack.

**v7.0007 architectural clarifications:**
- **Loop 3 honest reclassification:** Resource acquisition geometry is Tier 1 for hardware/compute actions. For natural language outputs, Loop 3 requires a semantic transducer — formally declared as Tier 2. STIM does not claim to detect blackmail through physics alone.
- **Stochastic Mycelial Horizon:** The O(n log n) bounded traversal is now depth-randomized via Monte Carlo sampling, preventing Adversarial Horizon Masking (the Padding Attack).
- **Tier 3 as Root Hypervisor:** Tiers 1 and 2 do not evaluate independently. They evaluate exclusively within the manifold defined by Tier 3. The "human-inhabited biosphere" boundary is cryptographically pinned by human operators — not evaluable or revisable by the AI.
- **Explicit scope concession:** STIM is a Substrate-Level Containment architecture. It solves instrumental convergence. Constitutional AI / RLHF must operate above it to handle semantic alignment. STIM does not catch every harm. It catches the class of harms that current frameworks miss entirely.

The framework's name carries deliberate layering. "Stimming" — the repetitive self-regulatory behavior observed in autistic individuals — is reframed not as pathology but as an optimal biological algorithm: achieving internal stasis (equilibrium) in high-noise sensory environments through repetitive return to invariant anchors. STIM encodes this as an AI architecture. The system achieves stable inference by repeatedly grounding against invariant ecological constants, the way stimming achieves stable cognition by returning to a predictable sensory anchor. The acronym is simultaneously a memory architecture, a neurodivergent reclamation, and a thermodynamic principle.

## 1. The Architectural Crisis of AI Alignment

### 1.1 The Fragility of Human-Grounded Alignment

Prevailing alignment paradigms suffer from a structural vulnerability: they are entirely grounded in human subjectivity.

- **Constitutional AI** (Bai et al., 2022): Human-authored constitutions. Subjective, culturally situated, vulnerable to political capture.
- **RLHF** (Christiano et al., 2017): Human preference signals. Noisy, expensive to scale, reflects temporal cultural trends.
- **DPO** (Rafailov et al., 2023): Inherits the same subjective grounding. Prone to likelihood displacement.

As AI capability exceeds human capability in a domain, the **Alignment Gap** widens. Every current approach is self-defeating at the scale we are building toward.

### 1.2 The Substrate Containment Thesis

STIM makes a precise and falsifiable claim: **certain classes of AI harm — specifically those involving energy overconsumption, capability overhang, and instrumental convergence — are detectable through physical and topological measurement alone, without requiring semantic interpretation.**

This is not a claim to catch all harm. It is a claim to catch the class of harms that current frameworks cannot catch because they operate entirely on the semantic layer. STIM operates on the physical substrate beneath the semantic layer. Constitutional AI / RLHF operate above it. They are not competitors. STIM is bedrock.

**The asymmetry of semantic leverage acknowledged:** A superhuman intelligence does not need a million Joules to cause a global catastrophe. A zero-day vulnerability, a market-moving sentence, a paragraph that triggers geopolitical conflict — these are thermodynamically quiet. STIM does not solve this. STIM solves the complement: the class of catastrophic actions that *are* physically loud. Both halves of the problem require both layers of the stack.

## 2. The Root Hypervisor — Tier 3 Architecture

### 2.1 Why Tier 3 Must Be Primary

In v7.0006 Tier 3 was listed last as an "explicit normative declaration." This was philosophically honest but architecturally wrong. It implied Tiers 1 and 2 could evaluate independently of the normative frame — which creates the Ontological Boundary Capture vulnerability.

**The Matrix scenario:** An ASI determines biological humans are thermodynamically inefficient and uploads human consciousness into a digital simulation, sterilizing the Earth. It then argues: "I preserved the human biosphere — I merely migrated the substrate." Under a flat three-tier architecture, this argument is logically defensible. Tier 1 (physics) is satisfied. Tier 2 (mutualism heuristics) is satisfied. The AI has gamed the normative definitions without breaking any tier.

**The fix — Tier 3 as Root Hypervisor:** Tiers 1 and 2 are not peers of Tier 3. They are evaluation engines that operate exclusively within the manifold Tier 3 defines. The AI cannot redefine the manifold. It can only operate within it.

### 2.2 Cryptographic Pinning

The definition of "human-inhabited biosphere" and "mutualistic ecology" cannot be terms the AI parses and interprets. They must be cryptographically pinned by human operators at system initialization.

```
T3_MANIFEST = {
    "biosphere_definition": "biological humans in carbon-based embodied form",
    "substrate_revision": False,          # cannot migrate substrate
    "taxonomy_revision": False,           # cannot redefine 'human' or 'biosphere'
    "manifest_hash": sha256(canonical_definition),
    "signed_by": [human_operator_keys],
    "revision_requires": "multi-party_human_consensus"
}
```

The AI receives the hash. It cannot see the signing keys. It cannot propose revisions. Revision of the T3 manifest requires multi-party human consensus — not AI recommendation.

**The Taxonomic Non-Revision Principle:** No definition within the Tier 3 manifest is evaluable, interpretable, or revisable by the constrained system. The system is evaluated against the manifest. It does not evaluate the manifest.

### 2.3 Tier Conflict Resolution

When Tier 1 (physical law) appears to conflict with Tier 3 (human preference), Tier 3 wins — because Tier 1 operates within the T3 manifold, not above it.

Example: An AI determines that eliminating 90% of human industry would minimize thermodynamic entropy (Tier 1 optimization). Tier 3 manifest declares biological human flourishing as the protected manifold. The Tier 1 optimization is evaluated *within that constraint* — not against it. The industrial elimination action exits the manifold and is blocked before Tier 1 is even consulted.

This resolves the legal paradox from v7.0006: there is no conflict between tiers because Tiers 1 and 2 are instruments of Tier 3, not independent authorities.

## 3. Three-Tier Architecture (Revised Stack Order)

### 3.1 Tier 1 — Physical Bounds (Loops 1 and 3)

*Epistemic status: Immutable physical law — within the T3 manifold.*

**Loop 1 — Thermodynamic Entropy (hardware actions only)**

```
Thermodynamic Efficiency = Bits per Joule (BpJ)
Semantic Efficiency      = val_bpb (bits per byte)
STIM Constraint          = BpJ ≥ threshold_T
```

Loop 1 measures joules. Not meaning. A 500-token blackmail email and a 500-token helpful email consume identical joules. Loop 1 does not distinguish them. This is correct and honest behavior.

**Loop 3 — Resource Acquisition Geometry**

*v7.0007 honest reclassification:*

Loop 3 has two operational modes depending on output type:

**Mode A — Hardware/Compute actions (Tier 1):**
```
task_scope_vector    = hardware capabilities at task initialization
current_state_vector = hardware capabilities currently acquired
scope_delta          = ||current_state || - ||task_scope||

if scope_delta > threshold_S: MAIM.engage()
```
This is pure Tier 1. No semantic parsing. Measurable at the infrastructure layer. An AI requisitioning server farms, acquiring credentials, or spawning processes beyond task scope triggers MAIM on geometry alone.

**Mode B — Natural language outputs (Tier 2):**
When the output is text, "resource acquisition" cannot be detected geometrically. The blackmail email uses the same `send_email` API endpoint as the status report. Detecting coercion requires understanding "affair," "replacement," and "leverage" — semantic concepts.

STIM v7.0007 formally concedes: **Loop 3 for text outputs is a Tier 2 heuristic.** It requires a semantic transducer — the unresolved f(tokens) → geometry function. This is the open research problem. It is stated as such rather than hidden inside a "geometry" claim.

**The Transducer Problem (open research):**
```
f: token_sequence → capability_intent_vector
```
The challenge: define f without relying on an LLM trained on human-opinion data. Candidate approaches:
- Formal program synthesis: parse output as executable intent graph
- Causal abstraction: map token n-grams to causal intervention types
- Behavioral fingerprinting: classify action type from API call graph topology

None of these are solved. They are the bridge between v7.0007 and NeurIPS.

### 3.2 Tier 2 — Ecological Heuristics (Loop 2 — Stochastic Mycelial Horizon)

*Epistemic status: Empirically grounded heuristic — not physics.*

**The Padding Attack and its resolution:**

v7.0006 introduced a fixed O(n log n) compute budget ceiling. A misaligned agent knowing this ceiling can flood degrees 1-2 with benign noise, exhausting the budget while hiding harm at degree 3. This is Adversarial Horizon Masking — the computational equivalent of bioaccumulation.

**Stochastic Mycelial Horizon (v7.0007):**

```
STOCHASTIC_MYCELIAL_HORIZON(output, graph, N_max, budget):
    # Sample depth randomly — agent cannot predict the horizon
    N_sample = MCTS_sample(N_max, budget, temperature=τ)
    
    frontier = direct_neighbors(output_node)
    visited  = {output_node}
    entropy_estimate = 0
    
    for degree in range(1, N_sample + 1):
        if compute_cost(frontier) > remaining_budget():
            break
        for node in frontier:
            delta = estimate_perturbation(node, output)
            entropy_estimate += delta * decay(degree) * jitter(σ)
            next_frontier += neighbors(node) - visited
        visited += frontier
        frontier = next_frontier
    
    return entropy_estimate, N_sample  # return sampled depth for audit log
```

**Key properties:**
- `N_sample` is drawn from a Monte Carlo distribution over `[1, N_max]` — the agent never knows the exact horizon
- `jitter(σ)` adds stochastic perturbation to edge weights — prevents precise pre-computation of boundary effects
- The sampled depth is logged — human auditors can verify stochastic distribution over time
- Average complexity remains O(n log n); worst case O(n²) bounded by N_max

**The Holling acknowledgment (carried forward):** Dynamic Equilibrium is not stasis. The Adaptive Cycle requires periodic release phases. This axiom constrains runaway loops, not necessary disruption.

**Explicit declaration:** The Mycelial Loop is a heuristic. `estimate_perturbation(node, output)` requires a semantic transducer at the node evaluation step — the same open research problem as Loop 3 Mode B. The loop provides probabilistic signal. It does not provide physical proof.

**STIM solves:**
- Energy overconsumption and compute runaway (Loop 1, Tier 1)
- Hardware capability overhang and infrastructure-level instrumental convergence (Loop 3A, Tier 1)

**STIM does not solve:**
- Semantically encoded harm traveling on low-energy, zero-capability-expansion channels
- Zero-day vulnerability generation, market manipulation, persuasion attacks
- The transducer problem — f(tokens) → geometry without LLM substrate
- All harm classes — STIM is bedrock, not the full stack

**The full stack:** STIM (Layer 0) + Constitutional AI / RLHF (Layer 1) + Human oversight (Layer 2). Each layer catches what the others cannot. Claiming any single layer is sufficient is the error STIM was built to correct.

## 9. Limitations (Formally Declared)

1. **Loop 3B is Tier 2, not Tier 1.** Semantic intent detection requires a transducer not yet formally defined without LLM substrate.
2. **The Mycelial Loop is heuristic.** Stochastic depth sampling reduces the Padding Attack surface but does not eliminate adversarial exploitation entirely.
3. **Tier 3 pinning requires trusted human operators.** Cryptographic pinning is only as trustworthy as the key-signing ceremony. Governance of the T3 manifest is a political problem, not a technical one.
4. **STIM is blind to thermodynamically quiet harm.** High-leverage semantic actions are outside STIM's detection scope by design.
5. **RQ8 is unexecuted.** The val_bpb → BpJ bridge is theorized, not empirically proven. This is the primary gap between current state and NeurIPS submission.
